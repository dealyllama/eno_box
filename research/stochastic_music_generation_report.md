     9|## Executive Summary
    10|This report synthesizes research findings to identify music generation techniques suitable for a portable, lightweight, and potentially embedded (ESP32/C++) implementation. We contrast high-complexity models with lightweight, port-ready algorithms.
    11|
    12|## TL;DR: Research Abstract
    13|
    14|The investigation focused on the trade-off between algorithmic richness and computational feasibility for resource-constrained environments. 
    15|
    16|**Key Findings:**
    17|* **High Viability (Target for ESP32):** Markov Chains and L-Systems emerged as the optimal candidates. Their logic is fundamentally state-based or rule-based string manipulation, requiring minimal RAM and no heavy math libraries. They can be implemented using simple arrays and transitions in C++.
    18|* **Moderate Complexity:** Gaussian Processes and context-free grammars offer more sophisticated textures but introduce significant overhead due to required matrix operations and recursive complexity, making them difficult to maintain on microcontrollers without advanced FPUs.
    19|* **Non-Viable (Excluded):** Transformer-based and ML models, while capable of high-fidelity generation, are excluded from this scope due to their extreme reliance on large-scale tensors and heavy-weight runtimes (e.g., PyTorch/TensorFlow), which are incompatible with the ESP32's memory and processing constraints.
    20|
    21|The research concludes that a decoupled architecture—where a lightweight C++ engine handles the stochastic logic while a Python prototype handles orchestration—is the most sustainable development path.
