     1|# Citation & Sourcing Standards
     2|
     3|Apply these standards to all research template responses.
     4|
     5|## Rules
     6|
     7|- **Citation format**: APA 7th edition inline `(Author, Year)` with a full References block at the end
     8|- **Source priority** (highest to lowest):
     9|  1. Primary: peer-reviewed journal articles, conference papers, original studies, official datasets
    10|  2. Secondary: review articles, meta-analyses, textbooks
    11|  3. Tertiary: encyclopedias, explainers, news — use only when primary unavailable, flag explicitly
    12|- **Links**: Always include a DOI (`https://doi.org/...`) or direct URL; use Google Scholar link as fallback
    13|- **Academic journals**: Identify and name the top journals in the relevant field; pull citations from them where possible
    14|- **Flagging**: Mark any claim without a locatable primary source as `[UNVERIFIED — no primary source found]`
    15|
